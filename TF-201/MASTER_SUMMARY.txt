================================================================================
                            KEY PHYSICAL INSIGHTS
================================================================================

1. PRESSURE DROP EQUATION:
   DP = 4 * f_c * (L_coil / D_h) * (rho * v^2 / 2)

   Where:
   - L_coil = N_turns * L_turn (total helical path length)
   - D_h = hydraulic diameter = 2*GAP*w/(GAP+w)
   - v = m_dot / (rho * GAP * w) (fluid velocity)

2. DOMINANT FACTORS:
   a) NUMBER OF TURNS (N = L / (w+t))
      - Reducing turns has the STRONGEST effect on DP
      - Each halving of turns roughly halves L_coil
      - Achieved by increasing pitch (w + t)

   b) FLUID VELOCITY (scales as v^2)
      - Velocity depends on flow area = GAP * w
      - Larger D_jacket increases GAP
      - Larger w increases channel area

   c) HYDRAULIC DIAMETER
      - Larger D_h reduces friction losses
      - Increases with both GAP and w

3. TRADE-OFF DISCOVERED:
   - Initial studies suggested larger w (channel width) is best
   - Extended studies revealed larger t (rib thickness) is MORE effective
   - This is because t directly reduces number of turns without affecting
     the flow area as much

================================================================================
                             CONCLUSIONS
================================================================================

1. The baseline design (DP = 8.56 kPa) exceeds the 8.5 kPa limit.

2. Through parametric optimization of channel and jacket geometry,
   pressure drop can be reduced by up to 96% to approximately 0.3 kPa.

3. The most effective parameter is RIB THICKNESS (t):
   - Increasing t reduces number of helical turns
   - Fewer turns = shorter flow path = lower DP

4. JACKET DIAMETER (D_jacket) has secondary importance:
   - Larger D_jacket increases annular gap
   - Increases flow area and hydraulic diameter

5. CHANNEL WIDTH (w) has diminishing returns:
   - Helps reduce velocity but also affects pitch
   - Optimal around 6-10 cm depending on t

6. Recommended design: Option C (Balanced)
   D_jacket = 55 cm, w = 8 cm, t = 25 cm
   Expected DP = 0.4 kPa (95% reduction)
