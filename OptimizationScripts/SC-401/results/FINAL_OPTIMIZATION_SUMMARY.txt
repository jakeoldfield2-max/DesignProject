================================================================================
                          KEY FINDINGS
================================================================================

1. DOMINANT PARAMETERS:
   - GAP has the strongest effect on pressure drop
   - Increasing GAP from 10mm to 22mm reduces DP by ~97%
   - Pressure drop scales approximately with v^2 (velocity squared)

2. PHYSICS EXPLANATION:
   - Larger GAP increases flow area, reducing velocity
   - Lower velocity dramatically reduces friction losses
   - DP = 4*f*(L/D_h)*(rho*v^2/2)
   - Doubling GAP approximately halves velocity, reducing DP by ~4x

3. TRADE-OFF OBSERVED:
   - Lower pressure drop comes at cost of reduced h_j
   - Heat transfer coefficient depends on Re^0.85
   - Lower velocity means lower Re, thus lower h_j
   - However, design remains valid because A_available >> A_required

4. DESIGN VALIDITY:
   - All optimized configurations passed design validation
   - Safety factor (A_av/A_req) ranges from 2.7x to 24x
   - Thermal performance is maintained even with reduced h_j

5. SENSITIVITY:
   - GAP: Most sensitive parameter (steep DP curve)
   - w: Moderate effect (affects pitch and number of turns)
   - t_wall: Weak effect (mainly affects coil geometry)
