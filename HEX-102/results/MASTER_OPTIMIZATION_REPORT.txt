================================================================================
                            KEY INSIGHTS
================================================================================

1. PRESSURE DROP DOMINATED BY TUBE SIDE
   - Tube-side dP: 627.88 Pa (99.98% of total)
   - Shell-side dP: 0.096 Pa (0.02% of total)
   - To reduce dP, focus on tube geometry

2. TUBE DIAMETER IS THE KEY PARAMETER
   - Larger diameter = dramatically lower pressure drop
   - Larger diameter = slightly higher volume
   - Optimal: 18-24 mm for volume priority, 26-36 mm for dP priority

3. TUBE LENGTH AFFECTS D/L RATIO
   - Longer tubes = better D/L ratio
   - Longer tubes = slightly higher volume
   - Optimal: 0.7-1.0 m for most applications

4. NUMBER OF PASSES MUST BE 2
   - N_p = 4 increases tube dP by ~4x
   - N_p = 6 increases tube dP by ~9x
   - Never use N_p > 2 if dP is a constraint

5. BAFFLE PARAMETERS HAVE MINIMAL IMPACT
   - Baffle spacing ratio: negligible effect
   - Baffle cut: negligible effect
   - Use TEMA standard values (0.5, 0.25)

6. PITCH RATIO HAS MINOR EFFECT
   - 1.25 (TEMA minimum) is optimal
   - Higher pitch slightly increases shell diameter
